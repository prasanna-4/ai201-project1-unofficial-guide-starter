Rate My Professors — Brookfield University
Professor: Raj Patel
Department: Computer Science
Course commonly reviewed: CS 310 Algorithms
Overall rating: 3.2 / 5  | Would take again: 54%  | Difficulty: 4.7 / 5

---
Review 1  (CS 310, Fall)  Quality 3 / 5  Difficulty 5 / 5
Patel is a brilliant researcher but his lectures are disorganized — he jumps between topics
and the board work is hard to follow. The exams are MUCH harder than the homework, which
catches everyone off guard. You have to read CLRS (the textbook) on your own to survive.

---
Review 2  (CS 310, Spring)  Quality 4 / 5  Difficulty 5 / 5
If you teach yourself from the textbook you will be fine; if you rely only on lecture you
will struggle. Homework is reasonable but the exams have a difficulty cliff. He clearly knows
the material at a deep level, he just is not a natural teacher. Recommend forming a study group.

---
Review 3  (CS 310, Fall)  Quality 2 / 5  Difficulty 5 / 5
Lectures felt improvised. The gap between homework difficulty and exam difficulty is the
biggest complaint — homework is a warmup, the exam is a marathon. Read CLRS chapters before
each unit. Curve exists but it is not generous.

---
Review 4  (CS 310, Spring)  Quality 4 / 5  Difficulty 4 / 5
Take this class only after you are comfortable with proofs. Patel assumes you can write a
formal induction argument. The content is fascinating if you can keep up. Do every textbook
problem, not just the assigned ones.
