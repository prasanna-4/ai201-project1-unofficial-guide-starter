Title: "CS 201 (Marsh) — how do I actually study for the exams?"

ORIGINAL POST:
Midterm 1 in Marsh's data structures is in two weeks and I'm panicking. The problem sets are
killing me and I don't know how to prep for the exam. Any advice from people who've taken it?

---
REPLY 1:
The single most important thing: her exams are 100% from the lecture slides, not the textbook.
Do not waste time grinding CLRS chapters she didn't cover. Re-derive every data structure she
drew on the board — linked lists, BSTs, heaps, hash tables — by hand, no IDE.

---
REPLY 2:
She hands out practice problems a week before each exam. They are nearly identical in format to
the real exam questions. If you can do the practice set cold, you'll be fine. Also: midterms
are curved so don't panic if the raw average is low.

---
REPLY 3:
Attendance is everything. The posted slides are skeletal — the actual content is what she says
out loud. If you missed lectures, borrow detailed notes from someone who didn't. Office hours
the week before the exam get packed, go early.

---
REPLY 4:
One trap: people assume the final is curved like the midterms. It is NOT. Plan your end-of-
semester effort accordingly — you can't bank on a final curve to save your grade.

---
REPLY 5:
Practice implementing a hash table and a balanced BST from scratch on paper, timed. That's the
kind of thing that shows up. Good luck!
