Rate My Professors — Brookfield University
Professor: Elena Marsh
Department: Computer Science
Course commonly reviewed: CS 201 Data Structures
Overall rating: 4.1 / 5  | Would take again: 78%  | Difficulty: 4.2 / 5

---
Review 1  (CS 201, Fall)  Quality 5 / 5  Difficulty 4 / 5
Professor Marsh is tough but completely fair. Every exam question comes straight from
the lecture slides, NOT the textbook, so if you go to class and actually take notes you
will be fine. The weekly problem sets are no joke though — budget 8 to 10 hours each.
Attendance matters way more than the readings. Would take again.

---
Review 2  (CS 201, Spring)  Quality 4 / 5  Difficulty 5 / 5
Heaviest workload of any class I took sophomore year. The problem sets are graded harshly
but she curves the midterms generously. Important: the FINAL is NOT curved, so do not coast
into finals week assuming a bump that isn't coming. I learned more from her than any other
professor in the department.

---
Review 3  (CS 201, Fall)  Quality 4 / 5  Difficulty 4 / 5
If you skip lecture you will fail. Exams are basically "did you attend and understand the
slides." She posts the slides after class but they are skeletal — the real content is what
she says out loud while drawing the trees and heaps on the board. Office hours are excellent
and she genuinely wants you to learn.

---
Review 4  (CS 201, Spring)  Quality 3 / 5  Difficulty 5 / 5
Brutal but I respect her. Be ready to implement balanced BSTs and hash tables from scratch
on a timed midterm. The textbook (CLRS) is barely used — everything is slide-driven. Do the
practice problems she hands out, they are nearly identical to exam questions.

---
Review 5  (CS 201, Fall)  Quality 5 / 5  Difficulty 4 / 5
Best professor in the CS department if you put in the work. Heavy problem sets, slide-based
exams, mandatory attendance in practice. Midterms curved, final not curved. Go to office
hours early in the semester before they get crowded.
